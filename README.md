In an ion thruster powered by a 400kV transformer, thrust is generated through a simple but powerful setup
that relies solely on electric current—no separate fuel supply is needed. The high-voltage transformer creates
an intense electric field. As the transformer powers up, this electric field interacts with the surrounding air,
stripping electrons from oxygen and nitrogen molecules and creating positively charged ions right from the
 ambient air. This process, known as ionisation, is the key to making the thruster work without additional
propellant.
Once the air molecules become ions, they respond to the electric field, accelerating away from the positively
charged electrode. This movement creates a directional flow of ions that propels the thruster forward. As the
ions are expelled, they generate a gentle but continuous thrust that can be adjusted by fine-tuning the voltage
level of the transformer.
This design takes advantage of the natural environment, using the transformer’s electric field to produce ions
and thrust without the weight or complexity of a separate gas supply. While the thrust is low, this approach is
highly efficient and ideal for certain applications where continuous, reliable propulsion is beneficial.
